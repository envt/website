Emilien Vallot

Senior Software Engineer with years of professional experience building C++ software, 3D graphics applications, and tools across Windows, Linux, and macOS.

about

Passionate about developing and automating things. It all started back in 2003.

At that time, I was already programming in assembly to make 4 levels of gray drawings on my HP40g calculator, but I wanted to become a 3D graphic designer.

- spoiler alert -

Unfortunately, it didn't work out and I thought to myself: Maybe I can develop tools for graphic designers?!

This explains my background, focused on digital imaging, computer vision, and later virtual reality.

Since then, I've been developing software and tools in several industries such as film, architecture, videography, robotics, or even professional audio.

I'm always looking for new challenges and things to learn.

When I'm not coding, I also enjoy reading, going to the movies, drawing, juggling, and photography!

career

All these years allowed me to gather a wide range of skills.

My first experience was at TechViz, during which my knowledge in C, C++, CPU, GPU, and 3D optimization in general (OpenGL, GLSL, linear algebra) greatly improved. I grew significantly in other areas such as TCP/UDP networking, hardware device integration, and low-level pipeline interception. Working within a team of about ten people made it an excellent first experience.

I then left for an amazing job at SolidAnim, working closely with graphics designers and helping them get their job done. I strengthened my C++, Qt, linear algebra, networking, and multi-threading skills. An extraordinary experience that allowed me to discover film sets and build advanced SLAM camera tracking systems in an R&D environment where we had full freedom to make impactful technical choices.

SolidAnim's relocation brought me to L-Acoustics, collaborating with passionate people always at the forefront of technology. Here I modernized my C++, CMake, Qt, and build systems knowledge, while bringing my 3D expertise to acoustic simulation rendering pipelines. I notably co-pioneered "Hive", a crucial GUI utility dedicated to AVB network device discovery and routing.

After a few years came an opportunity to return to pure rendering architecture at Abvent. The R&D team had complete ownership over technical choices, giving us full control of the development process (C++, CMake, Metal, OpenGL, CI/CD). Engineering state-of-the-art real-time GPU rendering pipelines for architecture visualization was something I deeply appreciated.

And here we are! I am currently at Fuzzy Logic Robotics, a dynamic startup where I began by managing the core 3D simulation engine codebase. Recently, my role evolved into Acting Tech Lead, steering a major strategic product shift by prototyping a brand-new automated robotic welding system from scratch and securing critical fundraising.
